## Anomaly Detection

What it is:
- Anomaly detection learns what normal examples look like and flags examples that look unusual.

Why it is used here:
- Tyre defects can be varied and incomplete. A good-only model tests whether defects stand out from normal tyre appearance.

Why not another approach yet:
- A complex method would be harder to debug before the data contract is stable.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- The baseline fits normal embeddings from good tyre images and scores validation/test images by distance from normal.

## Good-Only / Normal-Only Training

What it is:
- Training uses only examples labeled normal or good.

Why it is used here:
- The professor's direction is to learn normal tyre appearance from good tyres.

Why not another approach yet:
- Supervised classification already exists; this prompt adds a different research track.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- `D1_anomaly_train_normal.csv` contains only `target = 0` images.

## Feature Extractor Backbone

What it is:
- A backbone is a neural network used to convert images into useful features.

Why it is used here:
- A pretrained CNN can provide strong image representations without training a new deep model from scratch.

Why not another approach yet:
- Training a custom feature extractor would add complexity and require more validation.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- `src/anomaly/features.py` implements a ResNet embedding extractor.

## Frozen Pretrained CNN

What it is:
- Frozen means the CNN weights are not updated.
- Pretrained means the CNN already learned general image features from a large dataset.

Why it is used here:
- It is stable, explainable, and efficient for a first baseline.

Why not another approach yet:
- Fine-tuning could overfit and blur whether anomaly scoring itself is useful.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- `ResNetEmbeddingExtractor` removes the final classifier and freezes the encoder.

## Embedding Vector

What it is:
- An embedding is a numeric vector representing an image.

Why it is used here:
- Distance between embeddings gives a simple way to measure how unusual an image is.

Why not another approach yet:
- Pixel-level comparison is too sensitive to lighting and position.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- The pooled penultimate ResNet feature vector is used as the embedding.

## Mahalanobis Distance

What it is:
- Mahalanobis distance measures how far a point is from a distribution while accounting for feature covariance.

Why it is used here:
- It gives a principled distance from the good-only training distribution.

Why not another approach yet:
- It is simpler than deep anomaly methods and easier to explain.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- `MahalanobisScorer` fits mean and covariance precision from normal embeddings and scores new embeddings.

## k-Nearest-Neighbor Anomaly Score

What it is:
- kNN scoring measures distance to the nearest normal training embeddings.

Why it is used here:
- It is a lightweight alternative to Mahalanobis distance.

Why not another approach yet:
- It can be slower at large scale, so it is optional for now.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- `KNNScorer` stores normal embeddings and averages nearest-neighbor distances.

## Threshold Selection / Calibration

What it is:
- A threshold converts continuous anomaly scores into normal/anomaly decisions.

Why it is used here:
- Manufacturing inspection needs a decision point, not only a score.

Why not another approach yet:
- Choosing a threshold on test data would leak evaluation information.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- `select_recall_priority_threshold` chooses the threshold on validation only.

## False Positives vs False Negatives

What it is:
- False positive: a good tyre flagged as anomalous.
- False negative: a defective tyre missed as normal.

Why it is used here:
- False negatives are critical because missed defects are safety-relevant.

Why not another approach yet:
- Accuracy alone can hide dangerous false negatives.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX:
- Prediction CSVs include false-negative and false-positive flags.
