# Process Log 20260419_203935

This process log explains the concepts used in the web-curation prompt for a beginner.

## Web-Image Curation

What it is: selecting, tracking, filtering, and reviewing web-sourced image candidates before using them in research.

Why it is used here: TyreVisionX needs more likely-normal tyre images for anomaly detection, but web images are noisy and not automatically trustworthy.

Why not another approach yet: buying or collecting factory data may be better long term, but this prompt builds a careful research workflow first.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: query catalogs, candidate metadata CSVs, filters, anomaly-triage status, and human-review queues.

## Provider Abstraction

What it is: a common interface for different ways of finding candidate URLs.

Why it is used here: manual URL import works now, while future official search APIs can plug in later.

Why not another approach yet: direct scraping would be brittle and can violate terms.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: `CandidateProvider`, `ManualURLProvider`, and search-provider stubs in `src/web_collection/providers.py`.

## Search Query Engineering

What it is: designing search phrases that retrieve useful examples while avoiding irrelevant or defective images.

Why it is used here: good-only anomaly training needs clean, undamaged tyre candidates.

Why not another approach yet: random web search would produce too much noise.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: `configs/web_collection/query_catalog.yaml` has query text, view type, priority, positive keywords, negative keywords, and notes.

## Metadata Schema

What it is: a fixed set of columns that describe each candidate image and its processing state.

Why it is used here: the project must preserve provenance, download status, quality checks, review state, and future anomaly scores.

Why not another approach yet: loose filenames alone are not enough for research traceability.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: `CandidateRecord` and `CANDIDATE_COLUMNS` in `src/web_collection/schemas.py`.

## Deduplication

What it is: identifying repeated or near-repeated images.

Why it is used here: web search often returns the same product image many times, which can bias training.

Why not another approach yet: deep duplicate detection can come later; hashing is a strong first pass.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: exact SHA-256 content hashes and perceptual average hashes in `src/web_collection/filters.py`.

## Perceptual Hashing

What it is: a compact fingerprint that groups visually similar images even if file bytes differ.

Why it is used here: resized or recompressed copies of the same tyre photo may not share the same exact hash.

Why not another approach yet: learned image similarity is more complex and needs more validation.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: an 8x8 grayscale average hash and Hamming distance threshold.

## Quality Filtering

What it is: separating usable images from failed downloads, invalid files, tiny images, odd aspect ratios, or blurry images.

Why it is used here: anomaly models learn the normal distribution; bad images can teach the wrong normal.

Why not another approach yet: simple filters are transparent and easy to debug before training more complex quality models.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: `filter_candidates.py` writes kept, rejected, and review-needed CSVs.

## Blur And Resolution Checks

What they are: resolution checks enforce minimum image dimensions; blur checks estimate focus using Laplacian variance.

Why they are used here: small or blurry images may hide tyre surface details.

Why not another approach yet: these metrics are cheap, explainable, and good enough for a first pass.

How they are implemented in TyreVisionX: OpenCV and PIL inspect images during filtering.

## Anomaly Triage Vs Labeling

What it is: anomaly triage ranks or buckets candidates for review; labeling declares ground truth.

Why it is used here: an anomaly model can help prioritize likely-normal or suspicious web images, but it can be wrong.

Why not another approach yet: automatic labeling would contaminate the good-only training set.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: `score_candidates_with_anomaly.py` records pending or triage buckets and explicitly avoids final labels.

## Human-In-The-Loop Review

What it is: a person checks candidate images and assigns review statuses.

Why it is used here: human judgment is needed to approve likely-normal tyres and reject defects or irrelevant images.

Why not another approach yet: model-only review is not reliable enough for research-quality data expansion.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: review statuses, `review_queue_template.csv`, `review_guidelines.md`, and `build_review_queue.py`.

## Dataset Rights And Usage Caution

What it is: tracking source URLs and remembering that public images may have copyright or license limits.

Why it is used here: research prototypes and production datasets have different legal/data requirements.

Why not another approach yet: ignoring rights early creates future risk.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: source metadata is required, and docs label web data as research candidates.

## Why Web Data Can Help Anomaly Detection

What it is: web data can increase variety in normal tyre appearances.

Why it is used here: good-only anomaly models benefit from diverse normal examples.

Why not another approach yet: web data is helpful only after filtering and review.

How it is implemented in TyreVisionX: curated likely-normal candidates may later become additional normal training manifests.

## Why Web Data Can Hurt If Curated Badly

What it is: noisy web images can include defects, irrelevant objects, drawings, heavy edits, or duplicates.

Why it matters here: anomaly training on bad normal data can reduce sensitivity to real defects.

Why not another approach yet: the project first builds filters and review before scaling.

How it is handled in TyreVisionX: no automatic labels, quality filters, dedupe, anomaly triage, and human review.

## Future Multi-Product Anomaly Platform

What it is: a reusable framework where each product has its own queries, review rules, manifests, and thresholds.

Why it is used here: the professor's direction may later extend beyond tyres.

Why not claim it now: only tyre-specific prototype pieces are currently implemented.

How it is supported in TyreVisionX: generic metadata fields like `product_type`, provider interfaces, manifest contracts, and configurable filters.
