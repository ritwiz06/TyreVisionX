## Stronger Pretrained Backbones

What it is:
A stronger backbone is a larger or more modern image model used to convert images into feature vectors.

Why it is used here:
The first TyreVisionX anomaly baseline missed many defects, so stronger features may separate normal and defective tyres better.

Why not another approach yet:
Training a large model from scratch would be risky with the current dataset size.

How it is implemented:
The benchmark uses frozen ResNet18 and ResNet50 feature extractors with locally available pretrained weights.

## ResNet18 vs ResNet50

What it is:
ResNet18 is smaller and faster. ResNet50 is deeper and produces higher-capacity features.

Why it is used here:
ResNet18 is the reference baseline. ResNet50 tests whether stronger ImageNet features help tyre anomaly scoring.

Why not another approach yet:
ResNet models are already supported by the existing extractor and local weights exist.

How it is implemented:
`configs/anomaly/anomaly_benchmark.yaml` defines both ResNet18 and ResNet50 variants.

## Frozen Feature Extractor

What it is:
A frozen feature extractor uses a pretrained model without updating its weights.

Why it is used here:
It gives a stable baseline and avoids overfitting small D1 anomaly data.

Why not fine-tune yet:
Fine-tuning would add training complexity and needs careful validation.

How it is implemented:
`src/anomaly/features.py` creates frozen ResNet encoders and exports embeddings.

## Mahalanobis vs kNN

What it is:
Mahalanobis measures distance from one fitted normal distribution. kNN measures distance to nearby normal examples.

Why it is used here:
Tyre normal images may form multiple visual groups, so kNN may fit the data shape better.

Why not use a heavier method yet:
The project needs simple interpretable comparisons before PatchCore or other larger systems.

How it is implemented:
`src/anomaly/scorers.py` provides both scorers and the benchmark runs both.

## Threshold Sweep

What it is:
A threshold sweep tries many score thresholds on validation data.

Why it is used here:
Recall-critical inspection may need a different operating point than the initial normal-FPR-constrained threshold.

Why not tune on test:
Using test to pick thresholds would overfit the final evaluation.

How it is implemented:
`resnet18_threshold_sweep` chooses a threshold from validation scores and applies it once to test.

## Multi-Crop Inference

What it is:
Multi-crop inference evaluates several crops of the same image.

Why it is relevant:
Small tyre defects can be diluted in a whole-image vector.

Why not fully implement it here:
The prompt needed one low-risk local-feature improvement; patch-grid scoring was simpler to add safely.

How it relates:
Future prompts can extend the patch-grid implementation into multi-crop or multi-scale inference.

## Patch-Aware Embeddings

What it is:
Patch-aware embeddings score local image regions rather than only the whole image.

Why it is used here:
The likely failure mode is missing local cracks or subtle defects.

Why not a complex patch framework yet:
The repo should first test whether a simple local variant helps.

How it is implemented:
`resnet18_patch_grid_knn` scores the full image plus four quadrant crops and uses the most anomalous patch score.

## Bigger Models Are Not Automatically Better

What it is:
Model size alone does not guarantee better anomaly detection.

Why it matters here:
Tyre defects may be small, low contrast, and local. A larger global embedding can still miss them.

Why not blindly run every model:
That would create noisy comparisons and could overfit decisions to test results.

How it is handled:
TyreVisionX checks local readiness, runs supported variants only, and reports pending models honestly.

## Validation-Only Selection

What it is:
Model thresholds are selected on validation data, then test data is evaluated once.

Why it is used here:
It prevents overfitting the benchmark result to the test split.

Why not choose the best test threshold:
That would produce misleading research results.

How it is implemented:
Every executed variant calibrates threshold on `D1_anomaly_val_mixed.csv` and evaluates once on `D1_anomaly_test_mixed.csv`.
