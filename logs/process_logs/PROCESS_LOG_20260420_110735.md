## Corruption Robustness

What it is:
Corruption robustness measures how model performance changes when images are affected by realistic nuisance changes.

Why it is used here:
Tyre inspection images can vary because of lighting, focus, sensor noise, and compression.

Why not another approach yet:
Before adding heavier models, TyreVisionX needs to know whether the current best model is fragile under realistic capture changes.

How it is implemented:
`scripts/anomaly/run_corruption_benchmark.py` evaluates clean-trained anomaly variants on corrupted validation/test images while keeping the clean validation-selected threshold fixed.

## Evaluation Corruption vs Training Augmentation

What it is:
Evaluation corruption tests a fixed model. Training augmentation changes the normal training examples used to fit the anomaly scorer.

Why it is used here:
Keeping these separate shows whether the model is naturally robust and whether mild normal-only augmentation helps.

Why not mix them casually:
Uncontrolled augmentation can make the benchmark hard to interpret.

How it is implemented:
The corruption benchmark evaluates existing artifacts. The noise-robust variant separately fits kNN on clean plus mildly augmented normal embeddings.

## Gaussian Noise

What it is:
Gaussian noise adds small random pixel variation.

Why it is used here:
It approximates mild sensor noise or low-light capture noise.

Why not stronger noise:
Strong synthetic noise can hide cracks or create unrealistic texture.

How it is implemented:
`src/anomaly/corruptions.py` defines low and medium Gaussian noise settings.

## Blur

What it is:
Blur softens image details.

Why it is used here:
Tyre images may have mild focus or motion blur.

Why not stronger blur:
Extreme blur destroys defect evidence and is not a realistic baseline condition.

How it is implemented:
Low and medium Gaussian blur are included in the corruption config.

## JPEG Compression

What it is:
JPEG compression introduces block/artifact degradation.

Why it is used here:
Web images and some camera pipelines store compressed images.

Why not severe compression:
Severe compression would produce unrealistic artifacts and may erase small cracks.

How it is implemented:
The benchmark uses mild JPEG quality degradation.

## Brightness and Contrast Shifts

What it is:
Brightness changes overall lightness; contrast changes separation between dark and bright regions.

Why it is used here:
Lighting and camera exposure affect tyre surface visibility.

Why not extreme shifts:
Extreme lighting could make the image unusable and would not guide near-term model choices.

How it is implemented:
The benchmark includes darker/brighter brightness and lower/higher contrast settings.

## Clean Validation Threshold

What it is:
The model threshold is selected on clean validation data only.

Why it is used here:
This tests whether the deployed threshold remains stable under nuisance changes.

Why not corrupted test tuning:
Tuning on corrupted test data would overfit the evaluation.

How it is implemented:
Corrupted evaluation uses the threshold stored in each variant's `metadata.json`.

## Robustness Gap

What it is:
The robustness gap is the performance drop between clean test and corrupted test.

Why it is used here:
It shows whether noise or lighting causes missed defects to increase.

Why not only AUROC:
False negatives matter most for tyre inspection, so recall/FN gaps are emphasized.

How it is implemented:
`reports/anomaly/corruption_benchmark.csv` includes recall gap and false-negative increase relative to clean test.

## Too Much Noise Can Hurt Anomaly Detection

What it is:
In anomaly detection, augmentation expands what the model treats as normal.

Why it matters here:
If augmented normal images become too broad, the model may stop treating real defects as unusual.

Why not aggressive augmentation:
TyreVisionX needs high defect recall, so augmentation must remain mild and realistic.

How it is implemented:
The robust variant uses only light noise, slight blur, mild brightness shift, and mild contrast shift on normal training images.

## How This Helps TyreVisionX

What it is:
This robustness stage clarifies whether the next improvement should be robustness or local defect modeling.

Why it is used here:
The current best model is improved but still misses defects.

Why not jump directly to a new model:
The corruption benchmark shows the current model is moderately robust; the remaining problem is likely more about local defect evidence.

How it is implemented:
Reports recommend keeping mild robustness augmentation as a candidate setting while prioritizing patch-aware/local-feature ResNet50 next.
